Module 00

The Project

Free, Equal and Connected — who we are, what we do and why.

Libres, Iguales y Conectadas (LIC) is a federal program that strengthens the economic, political and community autonomy of LBTI+ people in Argentina.

It's not just training: it's organizing + support + international advocacy. Faced with state rollback and hate speech, the answer is for the community to organize.

"A collective response." · January–November 2026

Population
LBTI+ people in Argentina
Reach
Federal — 5+ prioritized provinces
Period
January 15 – November 30, 2026
The 4 components
01
Federal Diagnostic
Co-designed with provincial leaders. National survey, focus groups and community resource map.
02
Training Workshops
100 LBTI+ people. 12 sessions (6 virtual + 6 in-person). Tracks: Economic · Civic/Rights · Leadership.
03
Third Places
30 LBTI+ people. 3 in-person + 3 virtual sessions. Co-built safe spaces. Self-management kit at completion.
04
Mentorships
10 mentors + 20 mentees. Virtual format. 5 sessions per pair. Profile: political, business and community leaders.
The 3 phases
1
Months 1–3 · Completed
Federal diagnostic and co-design
  • Federal survey — 82 responses, 8 provinces
  • Grupos focales: Tucumán (presencial) y AMBA
  • Mapa de recursos comunitarios
  • Hoja de Ruta 2026–2028 en co-creación
2
Months 4–10 · Ongoing
Implementation
  • 12 training workshops
  • Mentorship program
  • 6 Third Places
  • In-person federal launch
3
Months 11–12 · Upcoming
Advocacy and closure
  • Shadow Report
  • Meetings with embassies
  • Final report for OutRight
  • Administrative closure
Specific objectives
SO1 — Federalization
Collaborative federal strategy for the next 3 years, based on real urgent needs from 5 prioritized provinces.
SO2 — Economic autonomy
100 LBTI+ people trained in life skills. 20 people receiving individual mentorships.
SO3 — Third places
30 LBTI+ people in safe spaces to express themselves, build relationships and receive mutual support.
SO4 — International advocacy
Shadow Report to highlight the impact of the crisis before international organizations and embassies.
